First Steps

Childhood & Inspiration

The music dream started early.

My mom, who was a musician, thought I would follow my brother: just sit back in my throne and march to the beat of the drums. No way. At five, I chose a pretty dress and lovely hair.

That's how I started playing the charming violin.

The right rythm

One of the most memorable concerts I had the pleasure to participate was the one at Rythmiske School with Trio Col Basso.

It was so simple: we started the first half playing music and talking a little bit about history. After the break, we talked about our life as a group. In the end, the positive feedback we received from the audience was overwhelming.
They said that our performance was "wonderful" and "so special".
It is nice to have someone to look at us not so critically but just as a path to fame. That is really hard to grab.

Back to Music

Passion Rediscovered

The phone rang.


My friend Tine was calling me to come play some music that she compose.


Well, that sounds legit for a experienced violinist — you might think.


The problem was the break of two and a half years during high school, when I stopped playing the violin.


Tine knew that I lost the joy in music so she wanted to help me get that back.


So, I said yes.


Got back to the violin and tuned my strings.

The dream came to life (again).


At the end of highschool, I walked with courage towards my old violin teacher and asked him if it was too late for me to be a violinist.


It would be difficult, he said.

But I'm an ambitious girl.


In order to do the exam trial for the Conservatory, I had to work really hard. Meaning that the 5 hour practise daily routine would come back.

Finally, within six months I could be ready to beat the auditions.


I put a lot of effort to achieve that goal.


At the end, I did it. I got in the Aarhus Royal Music Conservatory to study Classical violin

A Hard Goodbye

My talented composer friend, Tine, went away after three years.


Looking back, I can see her as one of the most special influencers in my life. She shaped me to pursuit the music career by helping me to rediscover the passion and pleasure in music.


I have a lot of happy memories of us playing together and enjoying those moments without any pressure.


It was hard to let her go after such a beautiful period in our lives.
